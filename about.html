About the Author

Robert Christopher Howard (a.k.a. Chris, Chris-a-ber, Stick, Slickybob, Ravenwolf, and let’s not forget... Man-Child) was born and raised in Fort Smith, Arkansas, and has called Northwest Arkansas home for the past 25 years, where he juggles the demands of corporate project management with the far nobler calling of being a devoted husband and cat daddy to nine wildly entertaining furballs.

A proud Gen Xer with a deep affection for retro video games, Cheetos-stained controllers, ’80s movies, and Saturday morning cartoons—Chris blends humor, heart, and hard-earned wisdom into his storytelling. Though he never considered himself a “reader,” or a “writer,” for that matter, this memoir became a mission: part nostalgic time capsule, part love letter to his family, and part coming-of-age tale that spans decades.

Life Lessons of a Man-Child is his first published book and the culmination of a long-overdue creative awakening. It’s a reminder that it’s never too late to revisit the past, heal what’s been buried, and create something meaningful, especially when the stories you tell help others feel seen, heard, and a little less alone.

When he’s not writing or gaming, Chris enjoys traveling abroad, hunting for the perfect vintage video game cartridge, and keeping all nine cats from staging a coup.
